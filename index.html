// The Setting

Most of Herzliya Pituach asks you to choose between the sea and a real home. This one does not.

Albert Einstein 6 sits on the second line to the sea inside LifePark, a low-rise gated compound built around mosaic walkways and Mediterranean gardens. The beach is a two minute walk, yet you come home to a private street, an underground garage, and a lift that opens almost into your living room.

It is not an apartment pretending to be a villa. Across two floors and roughly 450 square meters, it lives like a house: a garden that wraps the home, a library, a cinema room, a hidden Pesach kitchen, and a sukkah already wired with electric shades and a kosher roof. The kind of detail you only get when a family builds for itself.

"Buyers fly in expecting to compromise. They walk out asking when they can move their family in. That is what second-line-to-the-sea with a real garden does to people."
